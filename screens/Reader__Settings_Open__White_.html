CHAPTER FOUR

The Architecture of Silence

The library at the end of the world was not a place of dust and decay, but one of crystalline precision. Here, the echoes of forgotten thoughts resonated against walls carved from solidified light. I walked through the corridors, the soft thud of my boots the only disruption to a stillness that felt almost sacred.

Every shelf held a story that had never been told—not because they were secret, but because the language required to understand them had vanished along with the civilizations that spoke them. It was here I realized that knowledge was not just about accumulation, but about the space between what we know and what we feel.

I reached for a volume bound in what appeared to be shifting silk. As my fingers brushed the spine, a warmth radiated through my palm, a digital pulse from a dead era.
